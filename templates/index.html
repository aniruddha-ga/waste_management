Recycling Products Details


Paper Recycling

Paper recycling involves sorting, shredding, pulping, filtering, and drying used paper to produce new paper products. Some examples of recycled paper products include newspapers, packaging materials, cardboard, and tissues.


Plastic Recycling

Plastic recycling consists of sorting, cleaning, melting, and reprocessing plastic waste to create new products. Recycled plastic is used in various items such as bottles, containers, plastic lumber, textiles, and automotive parts.

Waste Hazards


Chemical Waste

Chemical waste, such as from industrial processes or household products, can pose serious hazards to human health and the environment if not managed properly. It may contain toxic substances that can contaminate soil, water sources, and air if improperly disposed of.

E-Waste

Electronic waste, or e-waste, refers to discarded electronic devices such as computers, smartphones, and appliances. E-waste contains hazardous materials like lead, mercury, and cadmium, which can leach into the environment if not recycled or disposed of correctly.
